Neural network for object recognition

A Belgium robotics company hired me to solve a problem: Crates filled with toys are coming by on a conveyor belt. Because the crates will go into an automated storage unit, they need to be labelled wih what is inside.
This is where my solution comes in. I have built a deep convolutional neural network (DCN) with Tensorflow and trained it with different pictures of the items.
On the factory flow a simple PC takes a picture of the contents of the crate. By running the DCN with this picture (inference) we can determine what items are in that crate.

Below, you can find a demo video I have made for the customer as a proof of concept. The demo only works with five different items. The final neural network in production can distinguish between 25 different items.
